Dog Breeds Pictures Description
Rottweier

The Rottweiler is a medium-to-large or large-sized domestic dog breed. Because their primary function was to herd livestock and pull carts filled with butchered meat to markets, the dogs were known in German as Rottweiler Metzgerhund, or Rottweil butchers' dogs.
Great Dane

The large dog breed known as the Great Dane originated in Germany. The Great Dane is a descendant of hunting dogs that were used in the Middle Ages to hunt deer and wild boar as well as serve as guard dogs for German nobility. Together with its relative, the Irish Wolfhound, it is one of the largest breeds in the world.
Cane Corso

Mastiffs of the Cane Corso breed originate from Italy. It is typically kept as a guard dog or companion dog, though it can also be used to guard livestock. It was once employed for cattle herding and large game hunting.
German Shepherd

The breed was developed by Max von Stephanitz using various traditional German herding dogs from 1899. It was originally bred as a herding dog, for herding sheep.
American Bulldog

The American Bulldog is a large, powerful mastiff-like breed that was employed as a guard dog, catch dog, and stock dog on farms and ranches. They are descended from the Old English Bulldog, which is thought to have been brought to America by working-class immigrants from England as early as the 17th century.
